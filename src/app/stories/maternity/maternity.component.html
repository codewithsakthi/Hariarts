Half Saree Ceremony : he half saree ceremony is a coming-of-age ceremony in South India that celebrates a girl's first menstruation and transition into womanhood. The ceremony is usually performed when the girl is 9, 11, or 13 years old. The ceremony signifies that the girl is ready for marriage and is mature enough to understand her responsibilities to her family and society. The ceremony involves the girl wearing a traditional outfit called a Langa Voni. The outfit is a skirt and sari outfit, also known as a half-sari. The girl receives gifts and is adorned with jewelry.The ceremony also includes relatives and family members tying the waistband or dora to keep the saree secure, and performing traditional prayers. The ceremony is celebrated like a wedding function.
